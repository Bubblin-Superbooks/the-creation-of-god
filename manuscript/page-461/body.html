The permanent fabric of plants is composed of only Carbon, Hydrogen, and Oxygen. The tissue of animals contains an additional element, viz., Nitrogen. Plants, as a necessary result of assimilating their inorganic food, decompose carbonic acid and restore its oxygen to the atmosphere. Animals in respiration continually recompose carbonic acid, at the expense of the oxygen of the atmosphere and the carbon of plants.
