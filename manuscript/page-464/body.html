Animal Life.

The animal, like the vegetable, is also composed of chemical elements, and by chemical analysis has been found to contain eighteen, as follows:

  • Of primary or vital importance: Oxygen, Hydrogen, Carbon, Nitrogen.
  • Of secondary importance, entering into the more solid structures: sulphur, phosphorus, calcium, sodium, chlorine, silicon, potassium, fluorine, magnesia, iron.
  • Accidental constituents: Magnesium, alumina, copper, and lead.
